University at Buffalo History of Esports

  • ECollegiate Conference Finalists

    Our number 1 conference seed College League of Legends roster defeated Miami Ohio to become a finalist in the ecollegiate conference. Prior to the season's preemptive cancelation due to COVID-19, our Bulls were projected to win the conference and compete in the national CLoL competition, League of Legend's version of the NCAA playoffs.

    March 14th, 2020

  • UB Smash Club Joins UB Esports

    UB Smash Club, originally a club in the undergraduate student association voted on this day to leave SA and join UB Esports. With this merger, UB Esports grew by almost 100 members.

    March 4th, 2020

  • Student Life Esports Task Force Formed

    Senior Associate Vice President Christina Hernandez formed a task force style committee in order to "provide the opportunity for its students to participate in electronic gaming... Often known as esports". This committee, independent from the Campus Living task force formed by Campus Living Director Tom Tiberi, would lead to a comprehensive recommendation to the University involving the hiring of full-time staff members, support for UB Esports, and the creation of numerous "Esports Arenas" on-campus, repurposed computer labs dedicated to video games.

    October 23rd, 2019

  • UB CS:GO Forms

    With a growing interest for competitive and community events for the game, a group of students volunteered to found the CS:GO club. The group has gone on to field teams in the CSL collegiate tournament, reaching national playoffs in its first year of competition.

    September 30th, 2019

  • UB Dota 2 Forms

    Denied club status from the undergraduate student association, a group of students seeking to form a Dota 2 club at UB joined UB Esports, forming the Dota 2 club. The group has gone on to host regular events for club members and field a Dota 2 team competing in collegiate level competition.

    September 24th, 2019

  • UB Esports Founded

    After years of independent gaming clubs operating without direct recognition, the Department of Media Studies head Tom Feeley approved for the founding of the University at Buffalo Esports, a student group dedicated to the competitive and community aspects of video games on-campus. The UB Overwatch and League of Legends clubs were the first to join the community.

    September 23rd, 2019

  • Campus Living Esports Task Force Formed

    Just prior to the founding of UB Esports, Campus Living Director Tom Tiberi ordered the creation of a task force style committee with the purpose and intent of building the university's first "esports arena" for resident utilage. This committee would go on to designate the Red Jacket lounges as the site for future construction, opening the lounge in the fall semester of 2020.

    September 8th, 2019

  • UB Overwatch Club Founded

    Within a packed room in Baldy Hall, the Overwatch club held its first meeting to a crowd of listeners. The club would go on to field multiple teams within the Tespa Overwatch League, compete in the AndBox/NYXL spring rally, and hold successful online and in-person events. Despite not getting recognized by the undergraduate student association, the group persisted for over a year and eventually joined UB Esports as a founding club.

    September 2nd, 2018

  • Heroes of the Dorm Finalists

    Within Blizzard's MOBA Heroes of the Storm, University at Buffalo team ImprobaBull Victory reached the finals of the then annual college competition known as "Heroes of the Dorm" by defeating Cal Poly Pomona 3 - 2 in the semi-finals. This acheivement came solely from student efforts, and without the supervision or direction of staff or faculty on-campus. Buffalo and University news celebrated the students' success; this milestone proved to be vital to the future success of gaming at UB.

    May 12th, 2018

  • Formation of League of Legends Club

    In the spring of 2016, a group of students formed the Facebook page Amumu's Friends, a page which became the foundation for the UB League club. Seeking recognition, the club initially formed as a subsidiary of undergraduate student association club SARPA. The group would go on to field teams in collegiate League tournaments and host tournaments of their own, holding a massive annual tournament at SARPA's UBCon, with the prize pool peaking at $1000 USD during UBCon XXX. The group would go on to join UB Esports as a founding club.

    Spring 2016
